### 1. 一种自监督心电图基础模型，用于赋能心血管疾病预测和遗传因素发现。

- **英文标题**: A self-supervised electrocardiogram foundation model for empowering cardiovascular disease prediction and genetic factor discovery.
- **期刊**: Nature communications (2026)
- **作者**: Siying Lin, Zhaoqi Li, Qifan Wu
- **类别**: 可穿戴/传感器 AI
- **相关性**: 8/10 — 自监督心电图基础模型，预训练大规模数据用于心血管疾病预测和遗传因素发现，AI为核心方法，符合临床预测模型与生理信号AI方向。
- **原文链接**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42045241/

**中文摘要**: 心电图（ECG）已被广泛应用于心血管疾病（CVD）的诊断。当前基于心电图进行心血管疾病预测的深度学习方法通常缺乏泛化性和可解释性，导致性能有限。为此，我们通过在多组心电图数据集上对超过一千万份12导联心电图进行预训练，开发了自监督心电图大规模基础模型（ECG-LFM）。为增强心电图表征能力，ECG-LFM以自监督方式整合对比学习与掩码语言建模，使模型能够同时捕捉心电图信号中的全局上下文信息和细粒度模式。通过微调实现对八种心血管疾病的预测，并在多个数据集上达到平均受试者工作特征曲线下面积（AUROC）为0.930，表明其性能优于现有方法。ECG-LFM导出的重要特征（EDFs）能够表征已知的心血管疾病生物标志物，体现了模型的高度可解释性。将EDFs应用于全基因组关联分析，识别出24个与心电图相关的显著单核苷酸多态性（SNPs）（P值<5×10⁻⁸，LD r²<0.01），其中包括8项新发现。通过孟德尔随机化评估EDFs对心血管疾病的遗传因果效应，发现2种心血管疾病与4种EDFs存在因果关系。总体而言，ECG-LFM为心血管疾病提供了准确预测，并为心电图研究提供了新的遗传学见解。

**英文摘要**: Electrocardiogram (ECG) has been widely used in the diagnosis of cardiovascular disease (CVD). Current deep learning methods for CVD prediction using ECG often lack generalizability and interpretability, resulting in limited performance. Here, we have developed a self-supervised Electrocardiogram Large-scale Foundation Model (ECG-LFM) through pre-training over ten million 12-lead ECGs from multiple ECG datasets. To enhance ECG representation, ECG-LFM integrates contrastive learning with masked language modeling in a self-supervised manner, enabling the model to capture both global contextual information and fine-grained patterns within ECG signals. It was fine-tuned to predict eight types of CVDs and achieved an average area under the receiver operating characteristic curve (AUROC) of 0.930 from multiple datasets, which demonstrates improved performance compared to existing methods. The important ECG-LFM derived features (EDFs) are able to represent known CVD biomarkers, indicating the high interpretability of ECG-LFM. Applications of the EDFs in genome-wide association study identified 24 significant single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) (P-value < 5×10 -8 , LD r 2  < 0.01) associated with ECG, including 8 novel findings. The genetic causal effects of EDFs on the CVDs were evaluated by Mendelian randomization, indicating 2 CVDs and 4 EDFs having causal relationships. Overall, ECG-LFM provides accurate prediction for CVDs and novel genetic insights for ECG.

### 2. 基于CT的机会性筛查以增加临床价值：我的操作方法

- **英文标题**: CT-based Opportunistic Screening for Adding Clinical Value: How I Do It.
- **期刊**: Radiology (2026)
- **作者**: Perry J Pickhardt, Mathew H Lee, Joshua D Warner
- **类别**: 医学影像 AI
- **相关性**: 8/10 — CT影像利用可解释AI进行机会性筛查，直接增加临床价值，属于医学影像AI核心应用
- **原文链接**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42048591/

**中文摘要**: 人们日益认识到，身体CT扫描蕴含丰富的心脏代谢信息，可被利用来为患者带来额外益处。然而，机会性CT筛查在常规临床实践中的实施一直受到繁琐且耗时的手动测量与主观评估的阻碍。如今，可解释的人工智能算法有望改变这一现状。大量CT扫描的获取进一步增强了机会性筛查的潜在影响力。在这篇"操作指南"中，作者简要概述了当前一些可"即时"获取的方法，同时更侧重于新兴的自动化解决方案。无论原始影像检查指征如何，检测出未预期的或症状前状态（如骨质疏松、心血管疾病、肌少症和肝脂肪变性）均可能引导预防性干预措施。结合多种心脏代谢CT生物标志物的综合模型可应用于生存预测以及生物衰老、虚弱、癌性恶病质、代谢综合征和骨折风险等评估。在临床报告方面，必须仔细考虑一系列后勤、精算和伦理问题。然而，若操作得当，我们相信机会性CT筛查能够增加显著价值、节省成本，并提供与即将到来的人工智能信息时代相称的新水平个性化精准医学。

**英文摘要**: There is a growing awareness that body CT scans contain rich cardiometabolic information that can be leveraged for additional patient benefits. However, the clinical implementation of opportunistic CT screening in routine practice has been hindered by valuable yet onerous manual measurements and subjective assessments. Explainable artificial intelligence (AI) algorithms are now poised to change this. The potential impact of opportunistic screening is further enhanced by the large volume of CT scans being obtained. In this "How I Do It" installment, the authors briefly outline some current approaches that can be obtained "on the fly," while focusing more on emerging automated solutions. Detecting unsuspected or presymptomatic conditions, such as osteoporosis, cardiovascular disease, sarcopenia, and hepatic steatosis, could lead to preventive interventions, regardless of the original indication for imaging. Composite models that combine multiple cardiometabolic CT biomarkers can be applied to survival prediction and assessment of biologic aging, frailty, cancer cachexia, metabolic syndrome, and fracture risk, among other factors. For clinical reporting, a range of logistical, actuarial, and ethical issues must be carefully considered. However, if executed properly, we believe that opportunistic CT screening can add substantial value, be cost saving, and provide a new level of personalized precision medicine befitting the dawning AI information era.
