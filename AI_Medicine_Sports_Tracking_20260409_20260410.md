### 1. 空间组学与人工智能在可临床应用的癌症生物标志物中的应用。

- **英文标题**: Spatial omics and AI for clinically actionable cancer biomarkers.

- **作者**: Nic G Reitsam
- **类别**: 医学影像 AI
- **相关性**: 8/10 — AI与空间组学结合用于癌症生物标志物的临床诊断，符合AI+生物标志物的具体预测任务。
- **原文链接**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41955187/

**中文摘要**: 将空间组学与人工智能相结合，有望推动生物标志物研究和诊断的发展，并可能揭示空间靶点生物学的机制性见解。通过在常规病理学中开发可扩展、可重复的定量方法，有助于弥合发现、验证与真实世界临床应用之间的差距。

**英文摘要**: Integrating spatial omics with artificial intelligence is likely to advance biomarker research and diagnostics, with the potential to pair mechanistic insight into spatial target biology. By developing scalable, reproducible quantification in routine pathology, it can help bridge discovery, validation, and real-world clinical implementation.

### 2. 基于自动化胸部X线摄影器官量化可解释的生物年龄：qCXR-bioage

- **英文标题**: Explainable Biological Age from Automated Chest Radiography-based Organ Quantifications: qCXR-bioage.
- **期刊**: Radiology. Cardiothoracic imaging (2026)
- **作者**: Yoosoo Chang, Suntae Park, Hyungjin Kim
- **类别**: 医学影像 AI
- **相关性**: 8/10 — AI用于医学影像（胸部X光）的定量分析，并用于预后预测，符合AI临床预测模型方向。
- **原文链接**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41954474/

**中文摘要**: 目的 本研究旨在利用胸部X线片中的定量器官指标开发一个可解释的生物年龄模型，并评估该模型对死亡率的预后价值。  
材料与方法 在一项针对韩国成年人（2006年1月至2020年12月）的回顾性队列研究中，研究者使用深度学习分割软件提取胸部X线片的定量生物标志物（肺部面积、肺气肿概率、主动脉直径、心脏面积和骨骼），开发了一个名为qCXR-bioage的生物年龄模型。

**英文摘要**: Purpose To develop an explainable biologic age model using quantitative organ metrics from chest radiographs and evaluate the model's prognostic value for mortality. Materials and Methods In this retrospective cohort study of Korean adults (January 2006-December 2020), the authors developed a biologic age model, called qCXR-bioage, using deep learning segmentation software to extract quantitative chest radiograph biomarkers (lung area, emphysema probability, aortic diameter, heart area, and bone density). Automated measurements and XGBoost-derived clinical variables were integrated using the Klemera-Doubal method and multivariable ridge regression for 65 980 healthy adults, and the model was independently validated in 257 004 individuals. Predictive performance was assessed using R 2 and root mean squared error for chronological age and the Harrell concordance index (C-index) for mortality. The age gap (difference between radiographic and chronological age) was analyzed for all-cause and cause-specific mortality using multivariable Cox and Fine-Gray models. Results A total of 257 004 participants (mean age, 40.7 years ± 9.1 [±SD]; 141 778 male [55.2%]) were included. qCXR-bioage strongly correlated with chronological age ( R 2 = 0.991 in male participants, 0.981 in female participants; root mean squared error < 2). After a median follow-up of 8.1 years, 1948 deaths were recorded. qCXR-bioage outperformed chronological age in predicting all-cause mortality (C-index in male participants, 0.769 vs 0.768 [ P = .04]; C-index in female participants, 0.767 vs 0.761 [ P = .001]). Accelerated aging (qCXR-bioage ≥ 0.5 year above chronological age) was associated with greater all-cause mortality (adjusted hazard ratio, 1.28 [95% CI: 1.11, 1.48; P = .001] in male participants and 1.28 [95% CI: 1.06, 1.56; P = .01] in female participants) after adjustment for chronological age and clinical confounders, with the strongest associations for cardiovascular mortality (hazard ratio, 1.73 [95% CI: 1.24, 2.41; P = .001] in male participants and 3.04 [95% CI: 1.37, 6.78; P = .006] in female participants). Conclusion Chest radiograph-based qCXR-bioage helped predict all-cause and cause-specific mortality.
