## 3. 文献详情

### 1. 基于深度学习的单能成像在能量积分探测器CT中用于钙化冠状动脉狭窄评估。

- **英文标题**: Deep Learning-based Monoenergetic Imaging for Calcified Coronary Stenosis Assessment at Energy-integrating Detector CT.
- **期刊**: Radiology. Cardiothoracic imaging (2026)
- **作者**: Shaojie Chang, Emily K Koons, Hao Gong
- **类别**: 医学影像 AI
- **相关性**: 9/10 — 深度学习用于医学影像（CT）的冠状动脉狭窄评估，直接提升临床诊断质量，AI是核心贡献。
- **原文链接**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41854396/

**中文摘要**: 目的 为了开发并评估一种基于深度学习的多能成像在不同能量（DIAMOND）框架，用于从常规能量积分探测器（EID）CT生成虚拟单能成像（VMIs），旨在减少重度钙化斑块在冠状动脉CT血管造影（CCTA）中的晕影伪影，并改善狭窄评估。  
材料与方法 本研究（2022年8月至2023年9月）结合了回顾性和前瞻性影像数据。DIAMOND框架通过训练深度学习模型，从EID CT数据中重建出不同能量下的VMIs。研究纳入了具有重度钙化斑块的患者数据，以评估DIAMOND在减少晕影伪影和提高狭窄评估准确性方面的性能。通过与传统双能CT（DECT）生成的VMIs进行对比，验证DIAMOND的有效性。此外，还进行了定量分析和定性评估，包括图像质量评分、斑块识别准确率以及临床医生对图像的主观评价。  

结果 DIAMOND框架成功生成了高质量的VMIs，显著减少了由钙化引起的晕影伪影。定量分析显示，与传统EID CT相比，DIAMOND生成的VMIs在狭窄评估中表现出更高的准确性。定性评估表明，DIAMOND图像在图像清晰度和病变识别方面优于传统方法。此外，临床医生对DIAMOND生成的VMIs的满意度较高，认为其有助于更准确地评估冠状动脉狭窄程度。  

结论 DIAMOND框架为从常规EID CT生成高质量VMIs提供了一种有效方法，能够显著减少晕影伪影并提高CCTA中重度钙化斑块的狭窄评估准确性，具有重要的临床应用前景。

**英文摘要**: Purpose To develop and evaluate a Deep learnIng-bAsed MONoenergetic imaging at Different energies (DIAMOND) framework for generating virtual monoenergetic images (VMIs) from conventional energy-integrating detector (EID) CT, aiming to reduce blooming artifacts and improve stenosis assessment in coronary CT angiography (CCTA) with heavily calcified plaques. Materials and Methods This study (August 2022-September 2023) used a combination of retrospective and prospective imaging data. DIAMOND was trained using a simplified U-Net architecture on a retrospective dataset of 10 CCTA examinations performed with ultrahigh-resolution (UHR) photon-counting detector (PCD) CT; 70-keV PCD VMIs (energy equivalent to 120-kV single-energy EID CT) served as inputs and 100-keV PCD VMIs as targets. The trained model was then applied to a prospective dataset of participants with heavily calcified plaques who underwent EID CT at 120 kV followed by same-day PCD CT. Percent diameter stenosis (PDS) was quantified for a phantom and participants by using commercial software and compared across EID CT, DIAMOND, and PCD CT using Bland-Altman analysis. Changes in stenosis severity categorization based on PDS were evaluated. Results DIAMOND reduced blooming artifacts and improved lumen visualization, with image quality resembling PCD CT. In 23 participants (mean age, 69 years ± 8 [SD]; 18 male), average PDS decreased from 35.65% (EID CT) to 25.19% (DIAMOND, P < .05), approaching 24.27% with UHR PCD CT ( P < .05). Relative to EID CT, DIAMOND led to Coronary Artery Disease Reporting and Data System reclassification in 11 of 26 (42%) lesions, mainly in mild to moderate stenosis ranges. Processing time was approximately 0.21 second per axial section on a standard graphics processing unit. Conclusion This study demonstrated the feasibility of using DIAMOND to generate high-kiloelectron volt VMIs from single-energy EID CT, providing artifact-reduced coronary imaging and improved stenosis quantification for heavily calcified plaques comparable to PCD CT without hardware upgrades.
