### 1. 基于多参数MRI的深度学习与影像组学评估早期宫颈癌淋巴结转移。

- **英文标题**: Multiparametric MRI-based Deep Learning and Radiomics for Evaluating Lymph Node Metastasis in Early-Stage Cervical Cancer.
- **期刊**: Radiology. Imaging cancer (2026)
- **作者**: Yu Hao Bao, Mei Ling Xiao, Yong Ai Li
- **类别**: 医学影像 AI
- **相关性**: 9/10 — 医学影像AI用于宫颈癌淋巴结转移的术前预测，AI是核心贡献，直接服务于临床诊断。
- **原文链接**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41931018/

**中文摘要**: 目的：开发一种基于多参数磁共振成像（multiparametric MRI）的影像组学模型以及深度学习-影像组学（DLR）融合模型，用于术前预测早期宫颈癌的淋巴结转移（LNM）。  
材料与方法：本研究为一项多中心回顾性研究（2020年1月至2022年12月），将早期宫颈癌患者的术前MRI数据分为训练集、内部测试集和外部测试集。对肿瘤及其周围区域的影像组学特征和深度学习（DL）特征进行提取与分析。

**英文摘要**: Purpose To develop a multiparametric MRI-based radiomics model and deep learning-radiomics (DLR) fusion model for preoperative prediction of lymph node metastasis (LNM) in early-stage cervical cancer. Materials and Methods In this multicenter retrospective study (January 2020-December 2022), preoperative MRI data from patients with early-stage cervical cancer were split into training, internal testing, and external testing cohorts. Radiomic and deep learning (DL) features of both the tumor and lymph node were extracted separately from the MRI scans. Multivariable logistic regression was used to construct predictive models for LNM based on tumor and lymph node radiomic features (Rad_T+LN) and based on radiomic and DL features from both the tumor and lymph node (DLR_T+LN). The models' effectiveness and clinical applicability were evaluated using receiver operating characteristic curves, calibration curves, and decision curve analysis. A two-tailed P value of <.05 was considered statistically significant. Results The overall dataset included 862 patients (median age, 53 years [IQR, 45-60 years]). Rad_T+LN resulted in areas under the receiver operating characteristic curve (AUCs) of 0.81 (95% CI: 0.76, 0.86), 0.79 (95% CI: 0.72, 0.87), and 0.77 (95% CI: 0.71, 0.82) in the training, internal testing, and external testing cohorts, respectively. DLR_T+LN achieved AUCs of 0.83 (95% CI: 0.76, 0.91) and 0.79 (95% CI: 0.74, 0.84) in the internal and external testing cohorts, respectively, and did not improve over Rad_T+LN ( P > .05). Both models demonstrated good calibration and positive net benefit on decision curve analysis. Conclusion Rad_T+LN and DLR_T+LN exhibited robust diagnostic performance for LNM prediction.
